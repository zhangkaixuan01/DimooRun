Pick the direction that best matches an enterprise AI runtime control plane. These are structural directions, not final pixels.

A. Operator Workbench

Recommended. Dense, calm, task-first console with stronger navigation grouping, persistent scope, clear status hierarchy, and restrained flat visual language.

B. Command Center

More dramatic and operations-heavy, with dark-first treatment and right-side live activity. Strong for demos, but riskier for long daily use and accessibility.

C. Progressive Modernization

Lowest-risk refresh: improve tokens, spacing, topbar, cards, and tables while keeping current page structure mostly intact. Faster but less transformative.

My recommendation

  • Choose A: it fits repeat operator workflows better than a marketing-style or demo-heavy UI.
  • Keep the current Vue/Naive UI foundation and redesign the shell, tokens, global component primitives, and high-traffic pages first.
  • Use flat, high-contrast surfaces with clear status color semantics and compact interactions.

Scope guardrails

  • No landing-page hero treatment inside the console.
  • No decorative gradients or oversized cards that reduce operational density.
  • No full component-library replacement unless a page requires it.
